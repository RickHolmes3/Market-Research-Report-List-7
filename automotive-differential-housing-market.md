<p><p>The automotive differential housing market includes two primary types: cast iron and ductile iron. Cast iron housings are known for their durability, cost-effectiveness, and ability to dampen vibrations, making them suitable for heavy-duty and traditional vehicles. Ductile iron, on the other hand, offers higher strength, flexibility, and resistance to wear and impact, making it ideal for modern, high-performance vehicles. Both materials are widely used, with cast iron being more economical and ductile iron providing enhanced performance and longevity in demanding applications.</p></p>
