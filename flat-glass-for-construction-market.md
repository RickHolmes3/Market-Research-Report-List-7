<p><ul><li>Rolled Glass Process</li><li>Float Glass Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The flat glass market for construction primarily features two key types: rolled glass and float glass. Rolled glass is produced by passing molten glass through rollers, creating a textured surface and varying thicknesses, suitable for applications like skylights and decorative windows. In contrast, the float glass process involves floating molten glass on molten tin, resulting in a smooth, distortion-free surface that is ideal for windows, facades, and mirrors, ensuring high optical clarity and uniform thickness.</p></p>
