<p><p>Audio transducers and buzzers convert electrical signals into sound, featuring two main types: electromagnetic and piezoelectric. Electromagnetic transducers utilize coils and magnets to create sound waves, commonly found in traditional speakers and buzzers. In contrast, piezoelectric buzzers use materials that deform when electric current is applied, generating sound through rapid vibrations, offering advantages in compact size and efficiency. Both types cater to various applications, from alarm systems to consumer electronics, enhancing audio output across different markets.</p></p>
