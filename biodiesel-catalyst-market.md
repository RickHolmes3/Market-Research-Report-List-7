Hydroxide catalysts, such as potassium hydroxide (KOH), facilitate transesterification by promoting the reaction between fats and alcohols. Sodium methylate, on the other hand, is a more commonly used catalyst that enhances the efficiency of biodiesel production. Both types are essential in optimizing the conversion process, influencing yield, reaction time, and overall production costs in the biodiesel manufacturing industry.</p></p>
