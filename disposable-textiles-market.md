<p><p>The disposable textiles market primarily comprises non-woven and woven fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are engineered by bonding fibers through chemical, mechanical, or thermal processes, making them ideal for single-use applications like medical gowns and sanitary products due to their lightweight and cost-effective nature. In contrast, woven fabrics are created by interlacing threads, offering durability and strength. While woven textiles can be reusable, their disposable variants find applications in sectors requiring high hygiene standards, such as food service and healthcare.</p></p>
