<p><p>Flat panel antennas for the satellite market are categorized into two main types: electronically controlled antennas (ESA) and mechanically controlled antennas (MSA). ESAs utilize advanced electronic systems to steer the antenna beam, enabling rapid adjustments and improved tracking of satellite signals. These antennas are preferred for their agility and efficiency. In contrast, MSAs rely on mechanical movement for beam positioning, often being simpler in design but slower in response. Both types serve distinct applications in satellite communication and navigation.</p></p>
