Laminating involves using the wax to create protective layers on food items, enhancing their shelf life and appearance. Coating refers to applying the wax to surfaces, providing barrier properties against moisture and contaminants. Hardening involves integrating the wax into formulations to achieve desired texture and firmness in products like confections. Each type plays a crucial role in improving food quality and safety.</p></p>
