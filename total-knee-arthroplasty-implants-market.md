<p><p>Total Knee Arthroplasty (TKA) implants are essential for treating advanced knee joint conditions. The market features two primary types: fixed-bearing and mobile-bearing implants. Fixed-bearing implants have a stationary femoral component that stabilizes the knee, offering reliability and increased longevity. In contrast, mobile-bearing implants allow for more natural knee movement with a floating polyethylene insert, enhancing stability and potentially reducing wear. Both types cater to varying patient needs, surgical techniques, and preferences in restoring knee function and alleviating pain.</p></p>
