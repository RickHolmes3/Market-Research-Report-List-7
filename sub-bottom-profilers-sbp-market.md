<p><p>Sub Bottom Profilers (SBPs) are marine geophysical tools used to map and analyze seabed geology by emitting sonar waves. The market for SBPs is categorized into low-frequency (20 kHz) and high-frequency (200 kHz) variants. Low-frequency SBPs penetrate deeper, making them ideal for exploring sub-seafloor structures and sediment layers, suitable for geological surveys and resource exploration. Conversely, high-frequency SBPs provide higher resolution images of the shallow seabed, effectively used for environmental assessments, engineering projects, and habitat mapping.</p></p>
