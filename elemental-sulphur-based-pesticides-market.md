<p><p>Elemental sulfur-based pesticides are available in two primary market types: dispersion and powder. Dispersion forms of sulfur are typically liquid preparations that allow for easy application and effective coverage on crops, enhancing their pesticidal efficacy. In contrast, powder formulations consist of finely milled sulfur particles, offering versatility in application methods, such as dusting or mixing with water for spray solutions. Both forms are valued in agriculture for their fungicidal properties and are popular choices in organic farming practices.</p></p>
