<p><p>Gait rehabilitation systems can be categorized into mechanical and computer-aided types. Mechanical systems utilize physical devices, such as robotic exoskeletons or treadmills, to assist patients in retraining their walking patterns through hands-on support and resistance. In contrast, computer-aided systems leverage advanced technology, such as virtual reality and motion capture, to create interactive environments that enhance motivation and provide real-time feedback. Both types aim to improve mobility and restore normal gait through tailored rehabilitation strategies.</p></p>
