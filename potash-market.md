<p><ul><li>Potassium Chloride</li><li>Potassium Nitrate</li><li>Potassium Sulfate</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The potash market consists of three main types: Potassium Chloride (KCl), Potassium Nitrate (KNO3), and Potassium Sulfate (K2SO4). Potassium Chloride is the most widely used due to its affordability and effectiveness as a fertilizer. Potassium Nitrate provides both potassium and nitrogen, making it suitable for high-value crops. Potassium Sulfate, while more expensive, is preferred for sensitive crops and soils requiring low chloride levels. Each type caters to different agricultural needs, impacting overall crop yield and quality.</p></p>
