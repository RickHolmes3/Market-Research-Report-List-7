<p><p>Electroless plating is an advanced technique used to deposit a layer of metal onto a substrate without the need for electrical current. This process involves chemical reactions that provide a uniform coating, making it ideal for complex geometries and non-conductive surfaces. The materials used in electroless plating include nickel, copper, gold, and palladium, among others. These materials are critical in various industries such as automotive, electronics, aerospace, and medical devices, where corrosion resistance, wear resistance, and enhanced conductivity are essential.</p><p>The Electroless Plating Materials and Services Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.7% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for high-quality finishes and advancements in coating technologies.
