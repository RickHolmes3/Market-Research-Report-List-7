<p><ul><li>Implantable Cardiac Pacemakers</li><li>External Cardiac Pacemakers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pacemakers market is divided into two primary types: implantable cardiac pacemakers and external cardiac pacemakers. Implantable cardiac pacemakers are surgically inserted devices that regulate heartbeats by delivering electrical impulses, typically used for patients with chronic heart rhythm disorders. External cardiac pacemakers, on the other hand, are temporary devices used to manage heart rhythm irregularities, often during medical emergencies or surgical procedures. Both types play a crucial role in enhancing patient outcomes and improving quality of life for those with cardiac conditions.</p></p>
