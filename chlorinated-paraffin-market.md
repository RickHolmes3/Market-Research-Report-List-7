<p><p>Chlorinated Paraffins (CPs) are chemical compounds used as flame retardants, plasticizers, and lubricants. CP-42, CP-52, and CP-70 refer to different grades based on chlorine content and chain length. CP-42 has 42% chlorine, offering flexibility and low viscosity, ideal for PVC applications. CP-52, with 52% chlorine, provides higher flame resistance and is used in coatings and adhesives. CP-70, with 70% chlorine, is highly flame-retardant and used in heavy-duty applications like metalworking fluids. "Others" include specialized grades tailored for niche industrial uses, expanding the versatility of chlorinated paraffins.</p></p>
